Yangruirui (Ron) Zhou, Ph.D.

Ph.D. Advisor: Douglas Densmore

Ph.D. Committee Members: Ayșe Coskun, Wenchao Li, Catherine Klapperich

Yangruirui (Ron) Zhou received his Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from Boston University (BU) in 2025. He received his Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Software Engineering (Elite Program) from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC) in 2020. He was honored with the Most Outstanding Student of UESTC (成电杰出学生) in 2019, the highest undergraduate honor awarded by UESTC.

At Boston University, Yangruirui worked with Professor Douglas Densmore. His research focuses on the development of tools for the verification, simulation, control, synthesis, and testing of synthetic biological systems. His Ph.D. research emphasized the computational formalization and foundational infrastructure required for future AI-assisted co-design of biological networks and microfluidic systems, as well as self-driving laboratory platforms. This work contributed to the emerging research direction of “Hardware–Software–Wetware Co-design,” as articulated by his advisor, Prof. Densmore. His long-term goal is to establish the first end-to-end automated design–validation–implementation toolchain for scalable multicellular biological computation and microfluidic systems, enabling future LLM-driven bio-hardware co-design platforms.
